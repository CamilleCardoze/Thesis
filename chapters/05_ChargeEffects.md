# Effects of the charge composition

In this chapter, we will evaluate the effects that different charge compositions have on the IPA distillate purity, the distillation time, and the IPA recovery.

To do this, besides the original one, we will choose five different charge compositions from which to calculate with:

- 10 mol% IPA in the charge mixture
- 30 mol% IPA in the charge mixture
- 50 mol% IPA in the charge mixture
- 75 mol% IPA in the charge mixture
- 90 mol% IPA in the charge mixture

We will observe the reflected effects of these mixtures in regards to:

- Efficiency
- Elapsed time

From which, efficiency was calculated with the following formula:

$\eta =\frac{n_{\mathrm{IPA,distillate}} \cdot x_{\mathrm{IPA,charge}}}{n_{\mathrm{IPA,charge}} \cdot x_{\mathrm{IPA,distillate}}}\cdot 100$

- $\eta$ = IPA recovery efficiency, in %
- $n_{\mathrm{IPA,distillate}}$ = amount of IPA recovered in the distillate, in mol or kmol
- $n_{\mathrm{IPA,charge}}$ = amount of IPA initially present in the charge, in mol or kmol
- $x_{\mathrm{IPA,charge}}$ = IPA mole fraction in the initial charge
- $x_{\mathrm{IPA,distillate}}$ = IPA mole fraction in the recovered distillate

We can observe that, as the percentage of IPA in the charge increases, it gets easier to extract and therefore, the efficiency is higher; however, time also increases when the amount of IPA makes up most of the mixture (≥50%). It can be concluded that distillate purity is not strongly dependent on charge composition, but rather, the elapsed time is.
